Tips with the Farm Credit Administration:

Borrowers: As a FCS borrower, you have certain rights - all of which are outlined on the page linked below. If you feel these have been violated, or there has otherwise been a violation of US law, you are advised to file a complaint. We can be reached at info-line@fca.gov. Please note that we do not mediate disagreements between borrowers and institutions and only intervene when existing regulations have been violated. The best way to file is to use the borrower complaint form at the included link.

FCS Employees: If you feel that there are major problems at your institution that merit the attention of FCA, including being asked to do something you feel is illegal, please get in touch. You can reach us at info-line@fca.gov.

*Please note that this is not legal advice. You are advised to consult a licensed attorney before taking any action.

Other Agencies:

We only regulate the Farm Credit System (FCS). For things unrelated to the FCS, please see the table below for relevant agencies:

*Please note that this is not legal advice. You are advised to consult a licensed attorney before taking any action.

Firm Type Who do I contact?
Banks For complaints with consumer products, you should file with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB). You can also contact the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC), Office of the Comptroller of Currency (OCC), or respective state banking regulator (if regulated at the state level).
Credit Unions For issues with a credit union, you should file a tip with the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA).
Payday Lenders, Other Finance Companies, and Bank/Credit Union Consumer Products You should file complaints with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) or get in touch with your state's department of finance (or relevant regulator).
Investment Advisors Investment advisers with assets exceeding $110 million and/or that advise an entity registered under the Investment Company Act must register with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Contact the SEC here. All other advisers register at the state level with their respective state securities administrator under the Uniform Securities Act. You can find a listing of state securities administrators here.
Brokerage Firms To file a complaint about a brokerage firm, please contact the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA). They also have a toll-free helpline for seniors.
Public Companies To file a regulatory tip about a public company, please contact the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). If applicable, check out the SEC Whistleblower Program which, if a tip leads to enforcement, may include a portion of the settlement.
Futures Brokerages Futures brokers and those operating pooled investment funds for commodity futures are required to register with the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) and obtain membership with the National Futures Association (NFA). File a CFTC tip or complaint here.
